Getting Started: Setting up a Custom GPT in ChatGPT

Setting up a custom GPT in ChatGPT allows you to tailor the AI for specific tasks, topics, or behaviors without needing to code. This feature is available to ChatGPT Plus, Team, and Enterprise users.

1. Access the GPT Builder:

  • Log in to your ChatGPT account.
  • In the sidebar, click on "Explore GPTs" or navigate directly to chatgpt.com/gpts or chatgpt.com/gpts/editor.
  • Click the "+ Create" button, usually found in the top right corner or within the "My GPTs" section.

2. Choose Your Creation Method (Configure Tab Recommended):

  • You'll see two tabs: "Create" and "Configure."
  • The "Create" tab offers an interactive AI assistant to help build your GPT through conversation. While it can be used for quick setups, it's often recommended to use the "Configure" tab for more precise control over instructions and settings.
  • For detailed customization, switch to the "Configure" tab.

3. Basic GPT Setup (Configure Tab):

  • Name: Give your GPT a clear and memorable name (limited to 50 characters). This name will appear in the sidebar of your main chat screen.
  • Description: Write a brief description of what your GPT does.
  • Instructions: This is the most crucial part. Provide detailed guidelines on how the GPT should behave, its functionalities, and any behaviors to avoid. You can use Markdown for better organization and highlight critical rules in ALL CAPS (e.g., "ALWAYS use markdown when formatting output").
    • Define its identity and goal (e.g., "You are an expert programmer whose goal is to help users write efficient and error-free code").
    • Set rules for interaction, such as when to use knowledge files or how to interpret commands.
    • Avoid putting side effects directly in the instructions; focus on behavior.

4. Add Advanced Customizations (Configure Tab):

  • Profile Picture: Upload an image or ask the GPT Builder to generate one for your GPT's icon.
  • Conversation Starters: Provide example prompts to help users begin a conversation with your GPT.
  • Knowledge: Upload files (e.g., PDFs, documents) to provide additional context or specific data for your GPT to reference. This is useful for content exceeding ~8,000 characters.
  • Capabilities: Enable or disable features like Web Browsing, DALLÂ·E 3 (image generation), and Code Interpreter based on your GPT's purpose. Web browsing is off by default and must be manually enabled.
  • Actions: For advanced users, you can set up custom actions to allow your GPT to interact with external services via APIs (e.g., fetching real-time information, integrating with other software).

5. Test and Refine:

  • Use the "Preview" panel on the right-hand side of the screen to interact with and test your GPT as you build it.
  • Refine the instructions and settings based on the GPT's responses to improve accuracy and relevance.

6. Publish Your GPT:

  • Once you are satisfied with your GPT's setup, click the "Publish" or "Update" button.
  • You can choose who to share your GPT with (e.g., "Only me," "Anyone with a link," or "Public").

7. Manage Your GPTs:

  • After creation, you can manage your GPTs from the "My GPTs" section, including options to copy links, view version history, duplicate, or delete them.
  • Regularly update your GPT with new information and adjust its settings to adapt to changing needs.
