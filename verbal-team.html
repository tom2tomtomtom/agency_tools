- **Clear:** Explains complex concepts simply; avoids jargon and technical terms.
- **Empathetic:** Understands and addresses the challenges of small business owners.

**Tone Adaptations:**
- **Informative Content (Blog Posts, Whitepapers):** Primarily knowledgeable and clear, with a friendly undertone. (e.g., "Let's demystify SEO together.")
- **Marketing Communications (Ads, Social Media):** More friendly and empathetic, with clear calls to action. (e.g., "Running a business is tough. We're here to make marketing easier.")
- **Customer Support (FAQs, Help Articles):** Highly clear and empathetic, focusing on solutions. (e.g., "We understand this can be frustrating. Here's a step-by-step guide...")

**Avoid:** Overly academic language, condescending tone, industry-specific jargon without explanation.

**EXAMPLE USAGE:**

**Example Input (from marketing collateral):**
"Review this brochure and our recent social media posts. Brand guidelines state: 'Use empowering language, avoid jargon.' Brochure uses 'synergistic solutions'. Social media post uses 'disrupting the paradigm'."

**Example Output (AI-generated communication audit snippet):**
**Communication Audit: Brochure & Social Media Posts**

**Finding 1: Jargon Usage (Brochure)**
- **Issue:** The phrase "synergistic solutions" in the brochure contradicts the brand guideline to "avoid jargon." This can alienate the target audience and reduce clarity.
- **Recommendation:** Replace with simpler, more direct language such as "collaborative solutions" or "integrated approaches that work together."

**Finding 2: Tone Inconsistency (Social Media Post)**
- **Issue:** The phrase "disrupting the paradigm" in the social media post, while impactful, may not fully align with the "empowering language" guideline if it comes across as overly aggressive or exclusive.
- **Recommendation:** Rephrase to emphasize empowerment and positive change, e.g., "Empowering change in the industry" or "Leading the way with innovative thinking."

**Overall Recommendation:** Ensure all content creators are familiar with the updated brand voice and tone guidelines, particularly regarding jargon and empowering language. Conduct a brief training session focusing on practical examples of what to avoid and what to embrace.
