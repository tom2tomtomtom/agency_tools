**EXAMPLE USAGE:**

**Example Output (AI-generated best practices guide snippet):**
**Best Practices: Creative Brief Writing**

**1. Standardized Brief Template:**
   - **Recommendation:** Utilize a single, mandatory creative brief template that includes all essential sections (e.g., Project Overview, Objectives, Target Audience, Key Message, Deliverables, Tone & Style, Budget, Timeline).
   - **Benefit:** Ensures consistency and completeness across all briefs.

**2. Clear Objectives:**
   - **Recommendation:** Objectives should be SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Provide 1-2 clear, primary objectives per brief.
   - **Benefit:** Aligns creative efforts with measurable business goals.

**3. Concise & Actionable Language:**
   - **Recommendation:** Avoid jargon and vague terms. Use clear, direct language. For example, instead of "Increase brand synergy," use "Increase brand awareness by 15% among Gen Z."
   - **Benefit:** Reduces ambiguity, allows creative teams to quickly grasp requirements.

**4. Training & Onboarding:**
   - **Recommendation:** Develop a mandatory training module for all new and existing team members on how to effectively write and utilize the standardized creative brief. Include examples of good and bad briefs.
   - **Benefit:** Ensures consistent application of best practices across the team.
